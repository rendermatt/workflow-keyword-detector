How fit scoring works

The app crawls a prospect's website, then asks Claude how well that company fits one of your features.

The pipeline

  1. You start a crawl from the dashboard's New Crawl button: pick a feature and upload a CSV of company domains. A bare domain like example.com is treated as https://example.com.
  2. The workflow first fetches the feature's documentation page so the model knows what the feature actually does.
  3. For each company, starting from its seed domain, it follows links across the same registrable domain (including subdomains) — breadth-first — gathering visible text from marketing pages, docs, blog posts, careers pages, and the like. (15-second timeout, honors robots.txt.)
  4. The feature documentation plus the crawled site text are sent to Claude, which returns a structured fit score (0-100), a tier, a plain-language summary, the concrete signals it found, and a recommended sales angle.
  5. Results are upserted per (feature, company), so re-crawling a feature refreshes the scores instead of duplicating them.
Bounded & polite. Each crawl stops after CRAWL_MAX_PAGES pages (default 50) or once CRAWL_CONTENT_CHARS of text is gathered, and skips URLs disallowed by robots.txt. The crawler doesn't execute JavaScript, so content rendered only client-side won't be seen. The exact judgment is controlled by the editable prompt.
